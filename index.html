  • Proper Ergonomics: Good chair, monitor at eye level, keyboard position
  • Mental Health Days: Take occasional days off to recharge
  • Hobbies: Maintain interests outside of work
  • Social Connection: Don't isolate - join VA communities, co-work occasionally
Managing Multiple Clients
  • Capacity Planning: Don't exceed 40-45 billable hours per week
  • Time Blocking: Assign specific hours to each client
  • Separate Tools: Use different browser profiles or apps per client
  • Priority System: Rank tasks by urgency across all clients
  • Buffer Time: Keep 20% of schedule open for unexpected tasks
  • One Client at a Time: Focus fully on one client's tasks before switching
  • End of Day Review: Check all clients have what they need before signing off

  • Work-Free Zones: Keep bedroom and dining area work-free

  • Vacation Time: Plan 1-2 weeks off annually, inform clients 1 month ahead
  • Emergency Flexibility: Clients who respect boundaries will understand family emergencies

15 Social Media Management Basics
Platform Overview
Facebook Best for: Community building, customer service, local businesses. Post 1-2x daily.

LinkedIn Best for: B2B, professional services, thought leadership. Post 2-3x weekly.
TikTok Best for: Young audience, trends, creative content. Post 1-3x daily.

  • Brand Voice: Understand client's tone (professional, casual, funny, inspirational)

  • Visual Quality: Use Canva for graphics, ensure images are high-resolution
  • Captions: Write engaging first line (hook), include call-to-action
  • Hashtags: 3-5 relevant hashtags (not spammy), research trending ones
  • Timing: Post when audience is most active (check analytics)

  • Meta Business Suite: Free for Facebook & Instagram scheduling

  • Hootsuite: Comprehensive management, paid plans

The LAST Framework:

  • A - Apologize: Express sincere empathy
  • S - Solve: Offer solution or escalate to supervisor
  • T - Thank: Thank them for bringing it to your attention

I've [action taken] and you should [expected outcome] by [timeframe]. Thank you for bringing this to our attention - it helps us improve. Is there anything else I can do to help?"
